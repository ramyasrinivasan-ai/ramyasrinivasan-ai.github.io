Project Overview

Led end-to-end business analysis and solution architecture for an IBM Watson-powered AI virtual assistant deployed across a large US healthcare network. The assistant handled symptom triage, appointment scheduling, medication inquiries, and billing navigation — replacing manual administrative workflows at scale.

The Problem

A large healthcare network was overwhelmed with repetitive administrative queries routed to human staff. Staff were spending the majority of their time on low-complexity interactions that could be automated, leaving insufficient capacity for complex patient needs.

The Solution

Architected a HIPAA-compliant IBM Watson Assistant with a dynamic knowledge base capable of resolving clinical queries in real time. Designed 60+ intents, 120+ entities, and complex multi-turn dialog flows that handled edge cases and escalation paths gracefully.

Technical Architecture

Engineered EHR data integration workflows including patient identifier standards, ANSI X12/EDI compliance rules, and data matching algorithms. Built real-time API connections between Watson and the network's scheduling, billing, and clinical record systems.

Validation

Conducted 25+ stakeholder interviews with clinicians and compliance officers. Developed and executed 100+ UAT test cases validating AI outputs against clinical accuracy benchmarks and HIPAA standards.
