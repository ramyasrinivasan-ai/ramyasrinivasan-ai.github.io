Academic · Machine Learning · Research
ML vs Deep Learning: Automotive Case Study

Overview

This analytical report moves beyond definitions to apply AI/ML concepts to real-world industry problems — a skill equally valued in academic research and professional practice. It examines two foundational approaches through verified, publicly documented industry applications in the automotive sector.

Case Study 1 applies customer churn prediction to an OnStar connected vehicle subscription service using SVM and gradient boosting — where structured tabular data and interpretability requirements make traditional ML the right tool. Case Study 2 applies autonomous vehicle perception to a fully driverless robotaxi service using CNNs and Deep Reinforcement Learning — where raw sensor data at scale makes deep learning the only viable approach.

Key Insight

Before this project, "more powerful" seemed like the right frame for deep learning vs. traditional ML. After completing it, the understanding shifted: each approach has a domain where it excels, and applying deep learning to a structured tabular problem would actually produce worse results than a well-tuned SVM — while being far more expensive to train and impossible to explain to a business team.

Academic · AI Ethics · Leadership
Architecting Fair Automotive AI

Overview

When a self-driving car fails to detect a pedestrian because the training data did not include enough diversity in lighting conditions, demographics, or weather scenarios — that is not a software bug. That is bias, built into the system long before a single car hit the road.

This infographic maps bias across four ML pipeline stages — data collection, data labeling, model training, and deployment — identifying how bias enters at each stage, why it is often invisible until it causes harm, and what it looks like when it reaches production in automotive systems.

The Guardrail Framework

Four design interventions grounded in current industry practice: diverse and representative dataset construction, professional bias auditing using IBM AI Fairness 360 and Google What-If Tool, continuous post-deployment monitoring for model drift and disparate impact, and human-in-the-loop review for high-stakes decisions.

Why It Matters

Unchecked AI bias is a business liability. The EU AI Act (2024) classifies AI in safety-critical applications as high-risk, requiring documented bias mitigation as a baseline requirement. Organizations without fairness practices built into their pipelines are already out of compliance.

Tools Used

NotebookLM · ChatGPT with DALL-E 3 · PowerPoint · IBM AI Fairness 360 · Google What-If Tool

Netflix Data Analytics — The $1B Content Strategy

Overview

This project demonstrates end-to-end data analytics skills — from raw data cleaning and preparation to exploratory analysis and visualization, and from business findings to strategic recommendations — applied to one of the world's most data-driven companies.

The 8,807-title Netflix dataset was analyzed across 12 columns spanning 2008–2021. Four cleaning steps were performed in Excel, three distinct visualizations were built to answer specific business questions, and findings were translated into four actionable recommendations — including pivoting toward original TV series for subscriber retention and deepening international content partnerships.

Key Findings

Movies represent 69.6% of the library vs. 30.4% TV shows. International Movies is the #1 genre at 2,752 titles. Content growth peaked in 2019 then declined due to COVID-19 production shutdowns. TV-MA and R ratings dominate — the family segment is significantly underserved.

Business Insight

Data-driven personalization influences over 80% of content watched and saves Netflix an estimated $1 billion annually. The recommendations section goes beyond describing what the data shows to prescribing what should be done — connecting data patterns directly to strategic action.

Tools Used

Microsoft Excel · Python · Kaggle dataset · PowerPoint · Google Drive · NotebookLM
