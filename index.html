ML vs Deep Learning: Automotive Case Study

Case Study 1 — GM OnStar: Customer Churn Prediction (ML)

General Motors applies traditional machine learning (SVM and gradient boosting) to its OnStar subscription service to predict which customers are likely to cancel. The structured, tabular nature of subscription data makes traditional ML ideal — and interpretability is critical so business teams can act on predictions.

Case Study 2 — Waymo Autonomous Driving (Deep Learning)

Waymo's fully driverless robotaxi service uses CNNs and Deep Reinforcement Learning to process raw camera, LiDAR, and radar data in real time. No human engineer could manually define features to distinguish a pedestrian from a signpost across millions of scenarios — deep learning learns these automatically.
